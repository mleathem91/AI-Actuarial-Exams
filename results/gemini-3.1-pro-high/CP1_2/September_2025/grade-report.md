## Persona Insights

### Strengths
- The model exhibits phenomenal business acumen and understanding of the CP1 framework, navigating all commercial contexts (Corporate Strategy, Reinsurance, Non-Life Pricing, Pensions, Regulation) effortlessly.
- The model systematically targets 3-4 distinct strategic pillars per 4-mark question, ensuring total breadth instead of overly focusing on narrow elements. 

### Weaknesses identified by the Harsh Marker
- The only consistent "weakness" is typical for highly structured AI responses: the official examiner's scheme sometimes includes "free" marks for extremely rudimentary steps (e.g. "Decide upon criteria at the beginning"). The AI jumps straight to advanced metrics and skips the trivial meta-steps occasionally.
- Sometimes in listing options for questions with low marks (e.g. 4 expansion options for 4 marks), it misses the secondary half-marks the examiner has scattered for describing the option further, simply because it structured the output optimally to exactly match 'Propose four options'. 

### Benefit of the Doubt given by the Generous Marker
- The Generous marker occasionally awarded full marks where the LLM used strong commercial terminology (e.g. Reputational Risk/ESG/Revenue Yield Management) that implicitly answered the more basic text in the scheme. This is perfectly in line with how human actuarial markers assess commercial awareness.
