- Unparalleled ability to rapidly recall lists (e.g., uses of surplus, reasons to replace a model, features of subordinated debt, stakeholders in a securitisation).
- Excellent structure, formatting, and readability, avoiding the brain-dump approach and separating answers logically.
- Perfectly matched the actuarial control cycle and standard mitigating techniques for standard products.

### Weaknesses identified by the Harsh Marker
- Occasionally failed to "read the room" regarding the exact constraint. For example, in Q4 when presenting to 15-year-olds, the LLM jumped straight into *financial topics* to discuss, missing the crucial consulting points like "assume limited knowledge," "keep it high-level," and "provide external links."
- Sometimes missed the deepest layer of "why", answering mechanically rather than drawing out the full chain of events (e.g. in Securitisation marketing, drifting from distribution issues into collateral risk).

### Benefit of the Doubt given by the Generous Marker
- Generously awarded marks when the LLM gave modern, highly sophisticated real-world answers (such as using the Claims and Underwriting Exchange (CUE) database for fraud mitigation) which are superior to the simplistic answers in the marking scheme ("ask for a police report").
